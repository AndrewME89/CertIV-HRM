Key legislation you will work with throughout this course includes:

## Work Health and Safety Act 2011

This act protects the health, safety and welfare of all workers and others in workplaces and work activities. 

## Fair Work Act 2009

This legislation governs Australia's employee/employer relationship by providing a safety net of minimum entitlements, enabling flexible work arrangements and fairness at work, and preventing discrimination against employees. It protects certain rights of workers, including the right to engage in industrial activities.5

## Racial Discrimination Act 1975

This act aims to protect people from discrimination based on their race, colour, or national or ethnic origin.6

## Sex Discrimination Act 1984

This legislation protects people from discrimination based on their sex, gender identity, intersex status, sexual orientation, marital or relationship status, family responsibilities, because they are or may become pregnant, or because they are breastfeeding.6

## Age Discrimination Act 2004

This act prohibits discrimination based on age in employment, education, accommodation, and the provision of goods and services. It protects both young and older people.6

## Disability Discrimination Act 1992

This act prohibits discrimination against people with disabilities in employment, education, access to publicly available premises, accommodation, sport, goods, services and facilities.6
