## 1. Purpose of Job Interviews

Primary Goal:

- Assess whether a candidate is suitable for a role
    

Key Principle:

- All questions should relate directly or indirectly to the candidate’s ability to perform the job
    

---

## 2. What Employers Can Ask

Questions are acceptable if they:

- Assess skills, experience, or competencies
    
- Relate to job performance
    
- Explore relevant behaviours
    

Examples:

- “How do you handle stressful situations?”
    
- “Can you describe your experience with [task/tool]?”
    

Rule:

The relevance of the question to the role is what determines whether it is appropriate.

---

## 3. What Employers Cannot Ask (Victoria – Legal Framework)

### Key Legislation:

- Equal Opportunity Act 2010 (Vic)
    
- Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)
    

---

### Equal Opportunity Act 2010 (Vic)

Employers must not request information that could be used for discrimination, especially if it is irrelevant to the role.

---

### Protected Personal Attributes

Questions about the following are generally prohibited:

- Age
    
- Gender
    
- Sexual orientation
    
- Ethnicity / race
    
- Religion
    
- Political views
    
- Pregnancy / family plans
    
- Disability (physical or mental)
    
- Marital or relationship status
    
- Carer responsibilities
    

---

### Examples of Illegal / Inappropriate Questions

- “How old are you?”
    
- “Are you in a same-sex relationship?”
    
- “What’s your ethnic background?”
    
- “What religion are you?”
    
- “Are you pregnant or planning a family?”
    
- “Who do you vote for?”
    
- “Do you have a disability?”
    

Why these are problematic:

- They do not relate to job performance
    
- They may indicate intent to discriminate
    

---

### Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)

Even if questions are asked:

- Employers cannot use that information to discriminate based on protected attributes
    

Applies to:

- Current employees
    
- Prospective employees (job applicants)
    

---

## 4. Exceptions (When These Questions May Be Allowed)

Questions about personal attributes may be lawful if:

- They are directly relevant to the role
    
- They are asked for a non-discriminatory purpose
    

---

### Example:

- Role requires heavy lifting  
    → Employer may ask about physical capacity or limitations
    

Why this is allowed:

- It directly relates to the ability to perform job duties
    

---

## 5. How Candidates Can Respond to Inappropriate Questions

Candidates are not required to answer discriminatory or irrelevant questions.

---

### Recommended Response Strategy:

1. Politely challenge relevance

- “Can you clarify how that relates to the role?”
    

2. Redirect conversation

- Focus on relevant skills and experience
    

3. Maintain professionalism

- Avoid confrontation while setting boundaries
    

---

### Key Principle:

Candidates should never feel pressured to provide information that could be used to discriminate against them.

---

## 6. Legal Rights of Candidates

- Protection from discrimination applies to job applicants
    
- Candidates may:
    

- Refuse to answer inappropriate questions
    
- Take legal action if discrimination occurs
    

---

## 7. [[Human Resources (HR)|HR]] Best Practice (Critical for Assessments)

As an HR professional, you must:

### Ensure Interview Compliance

- Only ask role-related questions
    
- Avoid protected attributes unless legally justified
    

### Promote Fair Hiring

- Focus on merit-based assessment
    
- Standardise interview questions
    

### Reduce Risk

- Avoid legal exposure (discrimination claims)
    
- Train hiring managers on lawful questioning
    

---

## 8. Quick Summary (Memorise This)

Interview questions must be relevant to job performance. Asking about personal attributes unrelated to the role may breach discrimination laws and expose employers to legal risk.

## Straight-Up Reality Check

If a manager asks “Are you planning to have kids?” in an interview, that’s not just unprofessional—it’s a legal liability waiting to happen.

From an HR perspective, your job is to:

- Shut that down
    
- Train people better
    
- Protect the company and the candidate
