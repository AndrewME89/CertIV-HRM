## 1. [[Human Resources (HR)|HR]] Legal Compliance (Core Concept)

### Why Legal Compliance Matters

HR is responsible for ensuring organisations comply with:

- Employment laws
    
- Anti-discrimination laws
    
- Workplace safety regulations
    

---

### Risks of Non-Compliance:

- Fines and penalties
    
- Lawsuits
    
- Reputational damage
    

---

### Key Principle:

HR acts as the “guardian of compliance” within an organisation.

---

## 2. Key Legal Areas in HR

---

### 2.1 Employment Laws

- Governs wages, hours, entitlements
    
- Example: Minimum wage, overtime
    

---

### 2.2 Anti-Discrimination Laws

- Prevent unfair treatment in hiring and employment
    

---

### 2.3 Workplace Safety ([[Work Health and Safety (WHS)|WHS]])

- Ensures safe working conditions
    
- Requires training, audits, and compliance
    

---

## 3. Challenges in Legal Compliance

- Constantly changing laws
    
- Different laws across regions/countries
    
- Balancing compliance with company culture
    

---

## 4. Employee Benefits (Strategic HR Tool)

---

### Why Benefits Matter

- Attract talent
    
- Retain employees
    
- Increase motivation
    

---

### Common Types of Benefits

- Health insurance
    
- Retirement plans (e.g. superannuation)
    
- Paid time off ([[Paid Time Off (PTO)|PTO]])
    

---

### Challenges

- High cost (especially healthcare)
    
- Catering to diverse workforce needs
    
- Poor communication/understanding
    

---

### Modern / Innovative Benefits

- Flexible work arrangements
    
- Mental health support
    
- Financial wellness programs
    
- Childcare/eldercare support

## 6. Global Expansion Models (VERY Important)

This is high-value content for assignments.

---

### 6.1 Subsidiaries

Definition:  
A separate legal entity owned by a parent company

---

#### Benefits:

- Legal protection (limits liability)
    
- Strong local presence
    
- Tax advantages
    
- Operational flexibility
    

---

#### Challenges:

- High cost and complexity
    
- Compliance with local laws
    
- Difficult cross-border management
    

---

## 6.2 Representative Offices

Definition:  
A non-revenue-generating local presence

---

#### Benefits:

- Low cost
    
- Market entry and research
    
- Brand visibility
    

---

#### Challenges:

- Cannot generate revenue
    
- Limited operations
    
- Regulatory restrictions
    

---

## 6.3 Employer of Record ([[Employer of Record (EOR)|EOR]])

Definition:  
A third party legally employs staff on behalf of a company

---

#### Benefits:

- Fast market entry
    
- Reduced legal risk
    
- Lower setup cost
    
- Flexible hiring
    

---

#### Challenges:

- Less control
    
- Dependence on provider
    
- Can become expensive long-term
    

---

## 6.4 Independent Contractors

Definition:  
Self-employed workers hired for specific tasks

---

#### Benefits:

- Cost savings (no benefits required)
    
- Flexibility
    
- Access to specialist skills
    

---

#### Challenges:

- Misclassification risk (legal issue)
    
- Lower engagement/loyalty
    
- Complex compliance rules
    

---
