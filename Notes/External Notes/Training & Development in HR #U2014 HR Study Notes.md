## 1. What is Training vs Development?

### Training

- Focus: Current job performance
    
- Purpose: Equip employees with specific skills and knowledge
    
- Short-term and role-specific
    

---

### Development

- Focus: Future roles and career growth
    
- Purpose: Prepare employees for advancement
    
- Long-term and strategic
    

---

### Key Distinction

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Training|Development|
|Short-term|Long-term|
|Job-specific|Career-focused|
|Immediate performance|Future capability|

---

## 3. Training Needs Analysis (TNA) — Critical Concept

### Definition:

A structured process to identify:

- Knowledge gaps
    
- Training priorities
    

---

### Methods:

- Surveys
    
- Performance appraisals
    
- Competency assessments
    
- Observation
    

---

### Key Insight:

Training without TNA is ineffective and wasteful.

## 6. Learning Styles

---

### Visual Learners

- Learn through images, diagrams, videos
    

Methods:

- Slides
    
- Infographics
    
- Mind maps
    

---

### Auditory Learners

- Learn through listening and discussion
    

Methods:

- Lectures
    
- Podcasts
    
- Group discussions
    

---

### Kinesthetic Learners

- Learn through hands-on experience
    

Methods:

- Role play
    
- Simulations
    
- Activities
    

---

### Key Insight:

Most people are multimodal → training should combine methods

---

## 7. Multimodal Training Approach

Effective training should include:

- Visual (slides, diagrams)
    
- Auditory (discussion, explanation)
    
- Kinesthetic (activities, practice)
    

---

### Practical Example:

- Presentation + group discussion + role-play

## 15. Best Practice Summary

Effective training should:

- Be based on TNA
    
- Follow structured frameworks (ADDIE)
    
- Use multimodal delivery
    
- Support continuous learning
    
- Align with organisational goals
    
- Be measurable and adaptable
    

---

## 16. Quick Summary (Memorise This)

Training improves current job performance, while development prepares employees for future roles. Effective training requires identifying skill gaps, using structured frameworks like ADDIE, delivering engaging learning experiences, and continuously evaluating outcomes to align with organisational goals.

## Straight-Up Reality Check

Bad training:

- No needs analysis
    
- Generic content
    
- No follow-up
    

Result:

- No behaviour change
    
- Wasted budget
    
- Disengaged employees
    

Good HR treats training like a system with inputs, outputs, and measurable outcomes.
