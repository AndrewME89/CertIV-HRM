## 1. What is Talent Acquisition?

Definition:  
Talent Acquisition (TA) is the strategic process of:

- Identifying
    
- Attracting
    
- Selecting
    
- Onboarding
    

…employees who meet both current and future organisational needs

---

### Key Insight:

Talent Acquisition is proactive and long-term—not just filling jobs.

---

## 2. Talent Acquisition vs Recruitment

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Talent Acquisition|Recruitment|
|Strategic & long-term|Short-term|
|Builds talent pipeline|Fills vacancies|
|Includes branding, planning|Focused on hiring|
|Ongoing process|Reactive process|

---

### Exam Line:

Recruitment is a subset of Talent Acquisition.

## 4. Talent Acquisition Strategy

---

### 4.1 Workforce Planning

Focus:

- Analyse current workforce
    
- Identify skill gaps
    
- Forecast future needs
    

---

### 4.2 Employer Branding

Definition:

- How your company is perceived as a workplace
    

---

Key Elements:

- Employee Value Proposition (EVP)
    
- Company culture
    
- Career growth opportunities
    

---

### 4.3 Sourcing Strategies

Common methods:

- Job boards (LinkedIn, Indeed)
    
- Social media recruiting
    
- Employee referrals
    
- Recruitment agencies
    
- Campus recruiting
    
- Talent pools
    

---

### 4.4 Selection Process

Best practices:

- Structured interviews
    
- Assessment tools (skills, psychometric tests)
    
- Panel interviews
    
- Cultural fit assessment
    
- Reference checks
    

---

### 4.5 Onboarding

Purpose:

- Integrate new hires
    
- Improve retention
    
- Reduce time to productivity
    

---

Key Steps:

- Pre-boarding
    
- Orientation
    
- Training
    
- Mentorship
    
- Feedback collection

## Straight-Up Reality Check

Bad hiring is expensive.  
Slow hiring is expensive.  
Hiring the wrong person is really expensive.

Good Talent Acquisition isn’t about filling roles—it’s about:

- Not hiring the wrong people
    
- Building pipelines before you need them
    
- Making people want to work for you
