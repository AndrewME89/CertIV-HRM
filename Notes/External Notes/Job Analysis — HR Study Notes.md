## 1. What is Job Analysis?

Definition:  
Job Analysis is the systematic process of collecting, analysing, and documenting information about a job.

---

### Purpose:

- Understand job roles
    
- Define responsibilities
    
- Identify required skills
    

---

### Key Insight:

Job Analysis is the foundation of almost all [[Human Resources (HR)|HR]] functions.

---

## 2. Why Job Analysis Matters

Job Analysis supports:

- Recruitment & Selection
    
- Training & Development
    
- Performance Management
    
- Compensation & Benefits
    
- Workforce Planning
    

---

### Core Principle:

You can’t hire, train, or evaluate someone properly if you don’t understand the job.

---

## 3. Key Components of Job Analysis

---

### 3.1 Job Description

Focus: The job itself

Includes:

- Tasks
    
- Duties
    
- Responsibilities
    
- Working conditions
    

---

### 3.2 Job Specification

Focus: The person

Includes:

- Skills
    
- Knowledge
    
- Qualifications
    
- Abilities (KSAs)
    

---

### Key Difference:

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Job Description|Job Specification|
|What the job is|Who can do the job|
|Tasks & duties|Skills & qualifications|

---

## 4. Job Analysis Process (5 Steps)

---

### Step 1: Identify Purpose & Scope

- Why are you doing it?
    
- Which roles are being analysed?
    

---

### Step 2: Select Data Collection Methods

- Choose appropriate approach:
    

- Observation
    
- Interviews
    
- Questionnaires
    
- Existing documents
    

---

### Step 3: Collect Job Information

Gather data on:

- Tasks and responsibilities
    
- Skills required
    
- Working conditions
    

---

### Step 4: Analyse the Data

- Identify patterns
    
- Define key job requirements
    
- Determine KSAs
    

---

### Step 5: Document Results

- Create/update:
    

- Job descriptions
    
- Job specifications
    

---

### Key Insight:

Clear documentation = consistent HR decisions

---

## 5. Methods of Job Analysis

---

### 5.1 Observation

What it is:

- Watching employees perform tasks
    

Pros:

- Accurate, real-time data
    

Cons:

- Time-consuming
    
- Not suitable for all roles
    

---

### 5.2 Interviews

What it is:

- Talking to employees, managers, experts
    

Pros:

- In-depth insights
    
- Captures soft skills
    

Cons:

- Can be biased
    

---

### 5.3 Questionnaires

What it is:

- Surveys to collect job data
    

Pros:

- Scalable
    
- Efficient
    

Cons:

- Depends on quality of responses
    

---

### 5.4 O*NET (Database Tool)

What it is:

- Standardised job database
    

Use:

- Benchmark roles
    
- Compare industries
    

---

### Key Insight:

Best practice = use multiple methods for accuracy

---
