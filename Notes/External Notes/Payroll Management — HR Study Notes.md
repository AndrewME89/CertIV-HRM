## 1. What is Payroll?

Definition:  
Payroll is the process of calculating and distributing employee pay, including wages, bonuses, deductions, and taxes.

---

### Key Functions:

- Calculate employee earnings
    
- Apply deductions (tax, super, etc.)
    
- Distribute pay
    
- Ensure legal compliance
    

---

### Why Payroll Matters

- Builds employee trust
    
- Ensures legal compliance
    
- Maintains business reputation
    

---

### Risks of Poor Payroll:

- Late pay → Low morale
    
- Underpayment → Legal breach
    
- Overpayment → Financial loss
    

---

### Key Components of Payroll:

- Basic Salary
    
- Allowances (e.g. transport, internet)
    
- Bonuses
    
- Deductions (tax, insurance, super)
    
- Net Pay (take-home pay)
    

---

## 2. Payroll Process (End-to-End)

### Step 1: Collect Employee Information

- Personal details
    
- Salary structure
    
- Bank details
    

---

### Step 2: Define Payroll Policies

- Leave and attendance
    
- Overtime
    
- Tax obligations
    
- Super / benefits
    

---

### Step 3: Track Time & Attendance

- Work hours
    
- Leave
    
- Overtime
    

---

### Step 4: Calculate Salary

Formulas:

- Gross Pay = Salary + Allowances + Bonuses
    
- Deductions = Tax + Super + Other
    
- Net Pay = Gross Pay – Deductions
    

---

### Step 5: Approval Process

- Payroll reviewed (e.g. Finance)
    
- Best practice: 2-level approval
    

---

### Step 6: Disburse Salary

- Bank transfer (most common)
    
- Automated payroll systems
    

---

### Step 7: Compliance & Tax Filing

- Submit tax obligations to authorities
    
- Late filing = penalties
    

---

### Step 8: Record Keeping

- Store payroll data (3–7 years)
    
- Includes payslips, tax records, contracts
    

---

## 3. What Does “Running Payroll” Mean?

Running payroll = executing the full pay cycle:

- Track hours
    
- Calculate pay
    
- Deduct taxes
    
- Pay employees
    
- File taxes
    
- Maintain records
    

---

## 4. Payroll Management Options

### Option 1: DIY Payroll

Best for: Small businesses (<5 employees)

Pros:

- Low cost
    
- Full control
    

Cons:

- Time-consuming
    
- High risk of errors
    

---

### Option 2: Outsource to Accountant

Best for: Small–medium businesses

Pros:

- Expert handling
    
- Reduced compliance risk
    

Cons:

- Cost
    
- Less control
    

---

### Option 3: Payroll Software / Provider

Best for: All business sizes

Pros:

- Automation
    
- Accuracy
    
- Scalable
    

Cons:

- Subscription cost
    

---

### [[Human Resources (HR)|HR]] Insight:

Most modern organisations use payroll software due to compliance complexity.

## 11. Quick Summary (Memorise This)

Payroll is the process of calculating, paying, and recording employee wages while ensuring compliance with tax and employment laws. Accuracy, timeliness, and compliance are critical to maintaining trust and avoiding legal risk.
