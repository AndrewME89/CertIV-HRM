Warehouse Team Member
Operations & Warehouse Level 2 — AQF / Award Full-time / Casual MEL · SYD · BNE $58,500 – $64,000 + super
Position purpose

Carry out safe, accurate, and efficient warehouse operations including receiving, storage, picking, packing, and dispatch of freight and goods in accordance with PPG's operational procedures and the relevant Award.

Key responsibilities
  • Receive and process inbound freight — verify against manifests, report discrepancies
  • Pick and pack orders to specification within KPI timeframes
  • Operate warehouse equipment including forklifts (licence required), pallet jacks, and RF scanners
  • Maintain accurate inventory records in the WMS (Warehouse Management System)
  • Follow all WHS procedures; report hazards, near-misses, and incidents promptly
  • Maintain cleanliness and organisation of allocated warehouse zone
  • Participate in stocktakes and cycle counts as required
  • Comply with PPG's Code of Conduct and all relevant policies
Selection criteria — essential
  • Current Forklift Licence (LF class) or willingness to obtain within 3 months
  • Demonstrated experience in a warehouse or logistics environment
  • Ability to work rotating shifts including early mornings, afternoons, and weekends
  • Strong attention to detail and accuracy in physical and system-based tasks
  • Ability to work safely in a physically demanding environment
  • Basic computer literacy (WMS/RF scanner experience desirable)
Selection criteria — desirable
  • Certificate II or III in Warehousing Operations (or working towards)
  • Experience with automated warehouse systems
  • Understanding of dangerous goods handling procedures
Key performance indicators
Pick accuracy
≥99.2%
Orders/shift
Target per roster
Incidents reported
100% (zero unreported)
Training compliance
100% on time
Reporting structure
  • Reports to: Warehouse Team Leader
  • No direct reports
  • Works alongside: 30–50 team members per shift per site
Working conditions
  • Physically demanding — standing, lifting, operating equipment
  • Rotating roster; shift work including weekends and public holidays
  • Covered by Road Transport and Distribution Award 2020
  • PPE provided; steel-cap boots required
Legislation & policy obligations
  • OHS Act 2004 (Vic) / WHS Act 2011 (NSW, Qld) — worker duties
  • PPG WHS Policy and Safe Work Method Statements
  • PPG Code of Conduct
  • Fair Work Act 2009 — NES entitlements apply from day one
Team Leader — Warehouse Operations
Operations & Warehouse Level 3 (Supervisory) Full-time MEL · SYD · BNE $72,000 – $80,000 + super
Position purpose

Lead a shift team of 10–15 warehouse staff to achieve daily throughput, accuracy, and safety targets. Act as the first point of contact for operational issues, people matters, and WHS concerns on shift.

Key responsibilities
  • Plan and allocate daily work across team members based on roster and volumes
  • Conduct daily pre-shift briefings including safety, targets, and communications
  • Monitor and report on KPI performance throughout the shift
  • Identify and resolve operational issues in real time; escalate as needed
  • Conduct on-the-job coaching and provide regular feedback to team members
  • Manage first-level attendance, conduct, and performance matters; escalate to HRBP
  • Complete incident reports and participate in investigations
  • Onboard and induct new team members in coordination with HR
  • Champion WHS culture; conduct hazard inspections and toolbox talks
Selection criteria — essential
  • Minimum 3 years warehouse experience with at least 1 year in a supervisory or leading hand role
  • Current Forklift Licence (LF)
  • Demonstrated ability to lead, motivate, and performance manage a team
  • Strong verbal communication and conflict resolution skills
  • Proficiency in WMS and basic reporting tools
  • Sound working knowledge of WHS obligations for supervisors
Key performance indicators
Team pick accuracy
≥99.2%
Shift throughput
±5% of daily target
Team engagement
Pulse score ≥65
Incidents on shift
100% reported & closed
Reporting structure
  • Reports to: GM Operations (site)
  • Direct reports: 10–15 Warehouse Team Members
  • Works closely with: HRBP, HSE Manager, Transport team
Development pathway
  • Certificate IV in Warehousing Operations or Leadership & Management (supported)
  • Target for PPG's High Potential Accelerator program (eligibility: 12 months in role)
  • Progression pathway: Senior Team Leader → Operations Supervisor → GM Operations
Legislation & policy obligations
  • OHS/WHS Act — supervisor duties of care
  • Fair Work Act — general protections awareness (do not take adverse action)
  • PPG Performance Management Policy — first-level case management
  • PPG Bullying, Harassment & Discrimination Policy
General Manager — Operations
Operations Level 5 (Senior Leader) Full-time Site-based (MEL/SYD/BNE) $155,000 – $175,000 + super + STI
Position purpose

Lead end-to-end warehouse operations across the site, delivering against safety, productivity, cost, and people metrics. Drive cultural accountability and change capability within a team of 120–200 staff.

Key responsibilities
  • Set and deliver site operational targets in alignment with PPG's strategic plan
  • Lead, develop, and performance manage a team of Team Leaders and supervisors
  • Own the site P&L for labour and operational costs
  • Drive the AI/automation change program at site level, including consultation obligations
  • Maintain senior WHS accountability (Officer duties under OHS/WHS Act)
  • Partner with HRBP on workforce planning, ER cases, and engagement strategy
  • Manage union relationships and ensure EA compliance at site level
  • Report monthly to COO on people, safety, and performance metrics
Selection criteria
  • 10+ years in logistics/supply chain with at least 5 in senior operational leadership
  • Proven track record managing large, diverse, unionised workforces
  • Strong financial literacy — ability to manage site labour budget
  • Experience leading organisational change in a technology/automation context
  • Tertiary qualification in Operations, Business, or Logistics (preferred)

  • PPG Change Management and Consultation Policy
HR Business Partner — Operations
Human Resources Level 4 (Professional) Full-time Melbourne HQ (travel to sites) $100,000 – $115,000 + super
Position purpose

Provide strategic and operational HR partnership to the Operations and Transport divisions (~284 staff). Translate business needs into people solutions across ER, workforce planning, performance, and change.

Key responsibilities
  • Manage and resolve complex ER matters including disciplinary processes, grievances, and terminations in line with Fair Work Act requirements
  • Partner with GMs and Team Leaders to address performance issues using PPG's performance management framework
  • Lead workforce planning for Operations — headcount modelling, succession, and gap analysis
  • Provide expert HR advisory on Award interpretation, EA compliance, and leave entitlements
  • Support change management for the AI/automation rollout — consultation, communications, reskilling coordination
  • Analyse people metrics (turnover, engagement, absence) and recommend interventions
  • Manage return-to-work for injured workers in collaboration with HSE and treating practitioners
  • Participate in WGEA data collection and gender equity action planning
Selection criteria — essential
  • Tertiary qualification in HR, IR, or Business (Cert IV HRM minimum; Bachelor preferred)
  • 3–5 years HR generalist experience with demonstrated ER case management
  • Sound knowledge of the Fair Work Act, NES, and relevant Modern Awards
  • Experience in blue-collar, logistics, or transport environments (desirable)
  • Confident influencing and coaching managers at supervisory to senior level
  • Strong data literacy — ability to interpret and act on HR metrics

HR Advisor
Human Resources Level 3 (Generalist) Full-time Melbourne HQ $78,000 – $88,000 + super
Position purpose

Deliver frontline HR advisory and administrative support across PPG's people processes — including recruitment coordination, onboarding, policy guidance, HRIS management, and first-level ER queries.

Key responsibilities
  • Coordinate end-to-end recruitment for operational and support roles — advertising, screening, interview scheduling, reference checks, offers
  • Manage onboarding and induction for all new starters including probation tracking
  • Respond to first-level HR queries from managers and employees on leave, pay, policy, and entitlements
  • Maintain accurate employee records in the HRIS; generate people metrics reports
  • Administer performance review cycles and maintain documentation
  • Support HRBP and CHRO with research, documentation, and project work
  • Assist with WGEA data compilation and policy register maintenance
Selection criteria
  • Certificate IV in HRM or Bachelor in HR/Business (studying towards accepted)
  • 1–3 years experience in an HR support or coordinator role
  • Solid understanding of the Fair Work Act, NES, and leave entitlements
  • High attention to detail; confident handling confidential information
  • Proficient in HRIS systems (Yardi / similar) and Microsoft Office
  • Strong written communication — able to draft letters, policies, and correspondence

Data Analyst — PPG Digital
Technology (PPG Digital) Level 3 (Technical) Full-time Melbourne HQ (hybrid) $95,000 – $115,000 + super
Position purpose

Transform operational and logistics data into actionable insights that support PPG's AI-led growth strategy. Collaborate with Operations, Commercial, and the broader tech team to build dashboards, models, and reporting solutions.

Key responsibilities
  • Build and maintain operational dashboards (Power BI / Tableau) for Operations and Commercial stakeholders
  • Analyse freight, route, and warehouse performance data to identify efficiency opportunities
  • Support the AI route optimisation project with data modelling and validation
  • Collaborate with HR on people analytics — turnover, engagement, workforce cost modelling
  • Maintain data quality in the data warehouse; document data lineage and definitions
  • Present insights to non-technical audiences including senior management
Selection criteria
  • Bachelor in Data Science, Statistics, Computer Science, or related field
  • 2–4 years data analysis experience; logistics/supply chain context desirable
  • Proficiency in SQL, Python or R, and BI tools (Power BI preferred)
  • Strong data storytelling skills — ability to translate complexity to clarity
  • Understanding of data privacy obligations (Privacy Act APPs) when handling employee data
