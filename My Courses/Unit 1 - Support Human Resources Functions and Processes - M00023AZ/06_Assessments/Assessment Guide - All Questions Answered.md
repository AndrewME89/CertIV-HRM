### Q1 — Key purpose of the Fair Work Act 2009 (~40 words)

**Your vault notes:** [[Key Legislation & Regulations#Fair Work Act 2009]] | [[National Employment Standards]]

**Key points to include:**
- Governs Australia's employee/employer relationship
- Provides a safety net of minimum entitlements (the [[National Employment Standards]])
- Enables flexible work arrangements and fairness at work
- Prevents discrimination against employees
- Protects the right to engage in industrial activities
- Administered by the [[Key Terms and Definitions#Fair Work Commission (FWC)|Fair Work Commission]]

---

### Q2 — The 11 NES entitlements + who they apply to (~40 words)

**Your vault notes:** [[National Employment Standards]]

**The 11 entitlements:**
1. Maximum weekly hours
2. Requests for flexible working arrangements
3. Casual employment (conversion rights)
4. Parental leave and related entitlements
5. Annual leave
6. Personal/carer's leave, compassionate leave & family/domestic violence leave
7. Community service leave
8. Long service leave
9. Public holidays
10. Notice of termination and redundancy pay
11. Fair Work Information Statement (FWIS) & Casual Employment Information Statement (CEIS)

**Who they apply to:** All employees in the national workplace relations system — regardless of their award, enterprise agreement or employment contract. Casual employees receive a subset only.

> ⚠️ Note: Superannuation is listed in your NES note — the assessment answer may count it as one of the 11. Cross-check your [[National Employment Standards]] note — the fairwork.gov.au source lists it.

### Q5 — Three key ethical obligations of HR personnel (~20 words each)

**Your vault notes:** [[01_Ethical Requirements]] | [[02_Working Ethically]]

1. **Privacy and confidentiality** — HR must apply the principles of the Privacy Act 1988, obtaining consent before collecting personal data and storing it securely
2. **Equal and fair treatment** — HR must ensure all recruitment, performance management, and employment decisions are free from discrimination and based on merit (EEO)
3. **Accuracy and integrity of information** — HR must ensure only accurate, verified information is used in employment-related decisions, maintaining current and correct records

### Q6 — Four features of an HRMS including analytics features (~50 words total)

**Your vault notes:** [[01_Technology to Collect Data and Information]]

1. **Centralised employee records** — stores personal details, job descriptions, qualifications and employment history in one secure system
2. **Payroll and time management** — tracks timesheets, leave balances, rosters and processes pay
3. **Performance management tracking** — records performance reviews, KPIs, and self-evaluations
4. **HR analytics and reporting** — enables analysis of workforce metrics such as turnover rates, absenteeism, engagement survey results, and skill gap identification to support strategic decisions

### Q9 — Define 'workforce sustainability' (~40 words)

**Your vault notes:** [[03_Sustainability Issues#^fe49c1]]

**Definition:**
Workforce sustainability refers to an organisation's capacity to maintain a capable, engaged, and motivated workforce over time. It encompasses staff retention, succession planning, workforce planning, developing employee competencies, sustainable leadership, managing employee relations, and ensuring employees can continue to meet the organisation's present and future needs.

### Task 3 — Improvement Opportunities

#### Two areas for improvement:

**1. Psychological inclusion and voice** — Statement 3 shows 84% feel neutral or unable to raise concerns. Despite a diverse workforce, employees are not feeling included or heard. Recommendation: implement structured feedback channels (e.g. regular 1:1s, anonymous reporting mechanism).

**2. Policy currency and enforcement** — Both the Code of Ethics and Access/Equity Policy are overdue for review (last reviewed Oct 2021). There is no evidence of D&I targets, no proactive representation goals, and no disability representation despite 0% in workforce data. Recommendation: review and update both policies to include proactive D&I commitments and targets.

#### Two potential barriers to change:

1. **Lack of management buy-in** — If middle managers don't model inclusive behaviour, cultural change won't occur. Barrier: managers may not see this as a priority.
2. **Resistance from staff accustomed to current culture** — 36% disagreed that CBSA is responsive to change (Statement 4). Changing workplace culture is slow and can face resistance.
