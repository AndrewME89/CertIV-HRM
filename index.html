Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)
The primary federal workplace law
FederalNESEmployment contracts
▾
Key obligations for recruitment

  • s.536 — Requirement to give Fair Work Information Statement to new employees on commencement.

Performance Management
Legal framework for managing, improving, and — where necessary — ending employment based on performance.
Fair Work Act — Unfair Dismissal (Part 3-2)
The two-part test: valid reason + procedural fairness
FederalDismissals.387
▾
Eligibility to claim
  • Minimum employment period: 6 months (12 months small business). Below high income threshold (~$175K p.a.).
The s.387 two-part test
  • Valid reason — genuine, sound, defensible reason related to conduct or capacity.
  • Procedural fairness — notified of reason, given opportunity to respond, support person allowed, response genuinely considered.
Procedural fairness checklist
  • Issue written notice of the performance concern
  • Give reasonable time to respond · Allow a support person at all formal meetings
  • Consider the response genuinely before deciding
  • Issue warnings progressively (except serious misconduct) · Document every step
PPG context
Aaliyah Wong is managing 4 active performance cases in Operations. Each needs a documented PIP, formal written warnings, and evidence of genuine opportunity to improve. Missing a procedural step — even with a valid reason — can make a dismissal unfair.
Fair Work Act — General Protections (Part 3-1)
Adverse action and workplace rights — reverse onus applies
FederalAdverse actionReverse onus
▾
Key risks in performance management
  • Placing an employee on a PIP shortly after they raised a complaint = likely adverse action claim.
  • Dismissing someone while on workers' comp or parental leave is extremely high risk.
  • Reverse onus — the employer must prove the adverse action was NOT taken for a prohibited reason.
PPG context
Operations staff are raising grievances. Any performance action against an employee who recently lodged a complaint must be documented to show the PIP was based on pre-existing data — not triggered by the complaint.
Disability Discrimination Act — Reasonable Adjustment
Obligation to adjust before managing performance
FederalDisabilityReasonable adjustment
▾
Intersection with performance
  • Before managing performance related to attendance or output, consider whether a disability or medical condition is a contributing factor.
  • Employer may be required to make reasonable adjustments (modified duties, adjusted targets, flexible hours) before taking performance action.
  • Failure to adjust can make a subsequent dismissal discriminatory — even if the performance reason is genuine.
PPG context
If a warehouse worker's performance declines due to a disclosed physical injury, PPG must explore modified duties before initiating a PIP. Jumping straight to disciplinary action is a DDA risk.
Employee Relations & ER
The legal architecture governing the employment relationship — bargaining, disputes, union rights, and termination.
Fair Work Act — Enterprise Bargaining (Part 2-4)
Good faith bargaining + BOOT + protected industrial action
FederalEnterprise AgreementBOOT
▾
Key concepts
  • Good faith bargaining (s.228) — attend meetings, disclose relevant information, genuinely consider proposals, no surface bargaining.
  • Better off overall test (BOOT) — every employee must be better off under the EA than the relevant Award. FWC applies before approving any EA.
  • Protected industrial action — employees may take action during bargaining if procedural steps have been followed (s.408–415).
  • Nominal expiry — EAs continue in force until replaced; parties can bargain from nominal expiry date.

PPG's EA expires June 2027. Union membership in Operations is 61%. Early preparation — understanding BOOT obligations, running employee listening sessions, and modelling pay against the Road Transport Award — is critical.

WHS & Compliance
Duties of care, incident management, and the regulatory framework that keeps workers safe.
Work Health & Safety Act 2011 (Cth) / OHS Act 2004 (Vic)
Primary duty of care — PCBUs, officers, and workers
Federal / StatePCBUOfficer due diligence
▾
Who has duties
  • PCBU (the business — PPG) — ensure health and safety so far as reasonably practicable.
  • Officers (directors, CEO, GMs) — exercise due diligence: acquire WHS knowledge, understand hazards, ensure resources, verify controls.
  • Workers — take reasonable care for own health and safety; comply with reasonable instructions.
Victoria note

Policy 05
Performance Management Policy
Owner: CHRO Review: Annual Applies to: All employees
Purpose

To provide a fair, consistent, and legally compliant framework for managing employee performance at PPG — focused on early intervention, genuine support, and clear documentation. This policy applies to both developmental performance management (improvement and growth) and formal disciplinary processes.

Principles
  • Early intervention — performance concerns should be raised promptly and informally before escalating to formal processes.
  • Genuine support — employees must be given reasonable opportunity, resources, and time to meet expectations.
  • Procedural fairness — every employee has the right to know the concern, respond to it, and have their response genuinely considered.
  • Support person — employees may be accompanied by a support person (not a practising lawyer) at any formal meeting.
  • Documentation — every step must be documented contemporaneously. Verbal conversations alone are insufficient.
Performance management pathway

Stage 1 — Informal coaching

Manager raises the concern directly and constructively in a 1:1 setting. Documents the conversation with agreed actions and a review date. No formal written record issued to employee at this stage.

Stage 2 — First written warning

If no improvement within the informal period (typically 2–4 weeks), HR and manager issue a formal written warning. The warning must: state the specific concern, reference the expected standard, outline the support being provided, and set a clear improvement timeframe (typically 4–8 weeks).

Stage 3 — Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

Structured 30–90 day plan with weekly check-ins. Must include measurable targets, support mechanisms, and consequences if not achieved. HRBP must be involved in all PIPs.

Stage 4 — Final written warning or termination

If PIP objectives are not met, PPG may issue a final written warning or proceed to termination. Both require CHRO approval. Termination letter must be issued in writing with a valid reason stated.

Serious misconduct (summary dismissal)

Where serious misconduct occurs (as defined in the Code of Conduct), PPG may terminate without notice or prior warning. HR must conduct an investigation before any termination. The employee must be given an opportunity to respond to the allegation.

Key risks to avoid
  • Skipping stages without documenting a genuine reason
  • Issuing a PIP immediately after an employee raises a grievance (adverse action risk)
  • Failing to provide a support person at formal meetings
  • Not considering reasonable adjustment for a disclosed disability
Legislation

Fair Work Act 2009 (s.387 — unfair dismissal; Part 3-1 — general protections) · Disability Discrimination Act 1992 · Fair Work Regulations 2009 (reg.1.07 — serious misconduct)

Owner: CHRO Review: Annual Applies to: All workers
Purpose

PPG is committed to a workplace free from bullying, harassment, sexual harassment, and discrimination. This policy reflects PPG's positive duty obligations under the Respect@Work Act 2022 and the Equal Opportunity Act 2010 (Vic) — to proactively eliminate these behaviours, not merely respond to complaints.

Definitions

Workplace bullying

Repeated, unreasonable behaviour directed at a worker or group of workers that creates a risk to health and safety. Examples: persistent criticism, exclusion, unreasonable workloads, humiliation in front of colleagues.

A single incident is not bullying — but may constitute harassment or misconduct.

Sexual harassment

Unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature that a reasonable person would anticipate could cause offence, humiliation, or intimidation. Includes verbal, written, or physical conduct. A single incident can constitute sexual harassment.

Discrimination

Treating someone less favourably, or imposing an unreasonable requirement on them, because of a protected attribute (see Policy 01 for full list).

PPG's positive duty obligations
  • Annual training for all workers (mandatory; completion tracked by HR)
  • Manager-specific training on early identification and response
  • Regular review of this policy and complaint data
  • Safe, confidential reporting pathways available to all workers
  • Zero tolerance for retaliation against anyone who makes a genuine complaint
Complaint process
  1. Report — employee reports to their manager, a different manager, the HRBP, or the CHRO. Anonymous reports can be made via the EAP (Employee Assistance Program).
  2. Acknowledge — HR acknowledges the complaint within 2 business days and advises the complainant of the process.
  3. Assess — HR assesses whether the matter requires informal resolution, formal investigation, or immediate interim action (e.g. separating parties).
  4. Investigate — formal complaints investigated by HR or an external investigator. Both parties interviewed; evidence gathered and documented.
  5. Outcome — findings communicated to relevant parties. If substantiated, disciplinary action taken against the respondent. If unsubstantiated, no adverse action against the complainant.
  6. Follow-up — HR checks in with complainant 4 weeks after resolution to ensure no recurrence or retaliation.

Policy 08
Grievance Resolution Policy
Owner: CHRO Review: Annual Applies to: All employees
Purpose

To provide employees with a clear, fair, and accessible process for raising and resolving workplace grievances — including concerns about management decisions, working conditions, policy application, or interpersonal conflict. PPG is committed to resolving grievances promptly and at the lowest appropriate level.

What is a grievance?

A grievance is any concern, complaint, or dispute raised by an employee about their employment at PPG. This includes but is not limited to:

  • Perceived unfair treatment or decisions by a manager
  • Application of PPG policies (e.g. leave, performance management)
  • Workplace conditions or changes to duties
  • Conflict with a colleague or manager
  • Concerns about underpayment or incorrect entitlements

Complaints relating to bullying, harassment, or discrimination are handled under Policy 06.

Resolution process

Step 1 — Informal resolution (Days 1–5)

Employee raises the concern directly with their manager in a private, respectful conversation. Both parties attempt to reach a mutually agreed resolution. If the concern is about the direct manager, the employee may approach the next level manager or HR.

Step 2 — HR involvement (Days 5–15)

If informal resolution is unsuccessful or not appropriate, the employee submits a written grievance to the HRBP or HR Advisor. HR acknowledges within 2 business days and facilitates a structured conversation between the parties.

Step 3 — Senior escalation (Days 15–30)

If unresolved, the CHRO reviews the matter and may bring in an independent facilitator or mediator. The CHRO's decision is final at the internal level.

External avenues

Employees retain the right to refer the matter externally at any stage to the Fair Work Commission (disputes about EA entitlements), the Fair Work Ombudsman (underpayments, NES), or the relevant anti-discrimination commission.

Principles
  • No adverse action — raising a grievance is a workplace right under s.340 of the Fair Work Act. No employee will be penalised for raising a genuine concern.
  • Confidentiality — grievances handled with discretion; disclosed only as necessary for resolution.
  • Timeliness — PPG aims to resolve all grievances within 30 calendar days of receipt.
  • Documentation — all steps, meetings, and outcomes documented and retained on the employee's HR file.
Legislation

Fair Work Act 2009 (s.340 — general protections; dispute resolution provisions in EA) · Fair Work Regulations 2009 · National Employment Standards
