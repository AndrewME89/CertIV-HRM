Recruitment & Selection
Laws governing how you attract, assess, and appoint workers — and what you cannot do along the way.
Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)
The primary federal workplace law
FederalNESEmployment contracts
▾
Key obligations for recruitment
  • s.44 — Employer must not contravene the NES; applies from day one of employment.
  • s.536 — Requirement to give Fair Work Information Statement to new employees on commencement.
  • s.340 — General protections: cannot take adverse action because someone has a workplace right (e.g. asking about salary).
The 11 National Employment Standards
  • Maximum weekly hours (38 + reasonable additional)
  • Flexible working requests · Parental leave · Annual leave (4 weeks)
  • Personal/carer's leave · Family & domestic violence leave (10 days paid)
  • Community service leave · Long service leave · Public holidays
  • Notice of termination & redundancy pay · Fair Work Information Statement
PPG context
Operations staff are covered by the Road Transport and Distribution Award 2020. Employment contracts must reflect Award minimum rates — not just the NES. Fair Work Information Statement must be issued on day one.
Anti-Discrimination Acts (Federal)
Age Discrimination Act 2004 · Sex Discrimination Act 1984 · Racial Discrimination Act 1975 · Disability Discrimination Act 1992
FederalSelection criteriaProtected attributes
▾
Key concepts
  • Direct discrimination — treating a candidate less favourably because of a protected attribute.
  • Indirect discrimination — a requirement that appears neutral but disproportionately disadvantages a protected group.
  • Key attributes: age, sex/gender, race, disability, pregnancy, religion, national origin, sexual orientation, marital/family status.
  • Selection criteria must be genuinely role-relevant. Interview questions must not elicit information about protected attributes.
PPG context
PPG's warehouse roles have historically attracted few women. Any physical selection criteria must be validated as genuinely necessary — otherwise PPG risks an indirect sex discrimination complaint.
Equal Opportunity Act 2010 (Vic)
Victorian state-level anti-discrimination — includes positive duty
VictoriaPositive dutyProactive obligations
▾
What it adds
  • Includes a positive duty — employers must proactively eliminate discrimination, not just respond to complaints.
  • Broader protected attributes than federal law, including physical features and political belief.
  • Complaints go to VEOHRC (Victorian Equal Opportunity and Human Rights Commission).
PPG context
Applies to PPG Melbourne HQ (224 staff). Positive duty means Dr. Priya Sharma must demonstrate proactive steps — structured interviews, panel diversity, documented selection decisions — not wait for complaints.
Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) — Australian Privacy Principles
APPs 3, 5, 6, 11 — candidate data obligations
FederalCandidate dataBackground checks
▾
Key APPs for recruitment
  • APP 3 — Only collect information reasonably necessary for the role.
  • APP 5 — Notify candidates at or before collection what you're collecting and why.
  • APP 6 — Don't use data for a purpose other than recruitment without consent.
  • APP 11 — Securely destroy unsuccessful candidate records once no longer needed.
PPG context
PPG conducts police checks and medicals for Transport roles. Candidates must be told upfront. Unsuccessful files should be destroyed after a defined retention period (commonly 12 months).
Performance Management
Legal framework for managing, improving, and — where necessary — ending employment based on performance.
Fair Work Act — Unfair Dismissal (Part 3-2)
The two-part test: valid reason + procedural fairness
FederalDismissals.387
▾
Eligibility to claim
  • Minimum employment period: 6 months (12 months small business). Below high income threshold (~$175K p.a.).
The s.387 two-part test
  • Valid reason — genuine, sound, defensible reason related to conduct or capacity.
  • Procedural fairness — notified of reason, given opportunity to respond, support person allowed, response genuinely considered.
Procedural fairness checklist
  • Issue written notice of the performance concern
  • Give reasonable time to respond · Allow a support person at all formal meetings
  • Consider the response genuinely before deciding
  • Issue warnings progressively (except serious misconduct) · Document every step
PPG context
Aaliyah Wong is managing 4 active performance cases in Operations. Each needs a documented PIP, formal written warnings, and evidence of genuine opportunity to improve. Missing a procedural step — even with a valid reason — can make a dismissal unfair.
Fair Work Act — General Protections (Part 3-1)
Adverse action and workplace rights — reverse onus applies
FederalAdverse actionReverse onus
▾
Key risks in performance management
  • Placing an employee on a PIP shortly after they raised a complaint = likely adverse action claim.
  • Dismissing someone while on workers' comp or parental leave is extremely high risk.
  • Reverse onus — the employer must prove the adverse action was NOT taken for a prohibited reason.
PPG context
Operations staff are raising grievances. Any performance action against an employee who recently lodged a complaint must be documented to show the PIP was based on pre-existing data — not triggered by the complaint.
Disability Discrimination Act — Reasonable Adjustment
Obligation to adjust before managing performance
FederalDisabilityReasonable adjustment
▾
Intersection with performance
  • Before managing performance related to attendance or output, consider whether a disability or medical condition is a contributing factor.
  • Employer may be required to make reasonable adjustments (modified duties, adjusted targets, flexible hours) before taking performance action.
  • Failure to adjust can make a subsequent dismissal discriminatory — even if the performance reason is genuine.
PPG context
If a warehouse worker's performance declines due to a disclosed physical injury, PPG must explore modified duties before initiating a PIP. Jumping straight to disciplinary action is a DDA risk.
Employee Relations & ER
The legal architecture governing the employment relationship — bargaining, disputes, union rights, and termination.
Fair Work Act — Enterprise Bargaining (Part 2-4)
Good faith bargaining + BOOT + protected industrial action
FederalEnterprise AgreementBOOT
▾
Key concepts
  • Good faith bargaining (s.228) — attend meetings, disclose relevant information, genuinely consider proposals, no surface bargaining.
  • Better off overall test (BOOT) — every employee must be better off under the EA than the relevant Award. FWC applies before approving any EA.
  • Protected industrial action — employees may take action during bargaining if procedural steps have been followed (s.408–415).
  • Nominal expiry — EAs continue in force until replaced; parties can bargain from nominal expiry date.
PPG context
PPG's EA expires June 2027. Union membership in Operations is 61%. Early preparation — understanding BOOT obligations, running employee listening sessions, and modelling pay against the Road Transport Award — is critical.
Fair Work Act — Redundancy (Part 2-2 NES)
Redundancy pay scale + consultation obligations
FederalRedundancyConsultation
▾
NES redundancy pay scale
  • 1–2 yrs: 4 wks · 2–3 yrs: 6 wks · 3–4 yrs: 7 wks · 4–5 yrs: 8 wks
  • 5–6 yrs: 10 wks · 6–7 yrs: 11 wks · 7–8 yrs: 13 wks · 8–9 yrs: 14 wks · 9–10 yrs: 16 wks · 10+ yrs: 12 wks
Genuine redundancy defence
  • Role is genuinely no longer required AND consultation obligations were met AND redeployment was genuinely considered.
PPG context
AI/automation in Operations could lead to role changes. PPG must consult, consider redeployment (reskilling is relevant), and pay NES entitlements. Skipping consultation exposes PPG to unfair dismissal claims even if the redundancy is genuine.

Key changes (2022)
  • Positive duty — must take reasonable and proportionate measures to eliminate harassment, not just respond.

WHS & Compliance
Duties of care, incident management, and the regulatory framework that keeps workers safe.
Work Health & Safety Act 2011 (Cth) / OHS Act 2004 (Vic)
Primary duty of care — PCBUs, officers, and workers
Federal / StatePCBUOfficer due diligence
▾
Who has duties
  • PCBU (the business — PPG) — ensure health and safety so far as reasonably practicable.
  • Officers (directors, CEO, GMs) — exercise due diligence: acquire WHS knowledge, understand hazards, ensure resources, verify controls.
  • Workers — take reasonable care for own health and safety; comply with reasonable instructions.
Victoria note
  • Victoria uses OHS Act 2004 (not the model WHS Act) — administered by WorkSafe Victoria. Terminology differs: "employer" not "PCBU"; "employee" not "worker". Core obligations are equivalent.
PPG context
Melbourne HQ operates under OHS Act 2004 (WorkSafe Vic). Sydney and Brisbane operate under the model WHS Act. PPG's HSE team (16 staff) must maintain state-specific compliance across all three jurisdictions.
Psychosocial Hazard Regulations (2023)
WHS Regulations — psychosocial risks now explicitly regulated
Federal / StatePsychosocial hazardsMental health
▾
What changed
  • Psychosocial hazards (excessive workload, bullying, poor support, role conflict, change anxiety) are explicitly regulated — employers must identify, assess, and control them.
  • Codes of practice: Managing Psychosocial Hazards at Work, Preventing and Responding to Workplace Bullying — not mandatory but set the standard.
PPG context
Operations staff face high overtime (8.4%), change anxiety from AI rollout, and low psychological safety (63/100). These are not just engagement issues — they are WHS risks PPG must formally assess and control.
Workers' Compensation Legislation (State-based)
Vic: WIRC Act 2013 · NSW: Workers Compensation Act 1987 · Qld: WCRA 2003
State-basedInjury managementReturn to work
▾
Key obligations
  • Must hold current workers' compensation insurance in each operating state.
  • Report notifiable incidents to the regulator (WorkSafe Vic / SafeWork NSW / WHS Qld).
  • Return to Work (RTW) — provide suitable duties; actively support injured worker's return in consultation with treating practitioner.
  • Terminating an injured worker while on workers' comp is heavily restricted and triggers adverse action risk.
PPG context
Transport and warehouse roles carry high physical injury risk. PPG must have state-specific RTW coordinators and documented RTW plans. Absence rate (3.2%) below national average — maintaining this requires active injury prevention and RTW programs.
Learning & Development
Regulatory and compliance obligations that shape how organisations train, credential, and develop their people.
NVE&T Regulator Act 2011 — ASQA & AQF
Registered Training Organisations and Australian Qualifications Framework
FederalRTOsQualifications
▾
Relevance to HR
  • Only RTOs can issue nationally recognised qualifications and statements of attainment.
  • AQF governs qualification levels — Cert I through IV, Diploma, degree, etc.
  • Training needs analysis should map skills gaps to AQF-recognised units where possible to give employees portable credentials.
PPG context
PPG spends $1,240/FTE on L&D. As warehouse automation rolls out, reskilling should lead to AQF credentials (e.g. Cert III in Warehousing Operations) — making investment portable and meaningful to workers, improving engagement and reducing change resistance.
WHS Act — Training as a Legal Obligation
Duty to provide information, training, instruction & supervision
Federal / StateMandatory trainingCompetency records
▾
Key obligations
  • Training must be role-specific, documented, and repeated when roles, tasks, or hazards change.
  • High-risk work licences (forklift, EWP, crane) are legally required — operating without one is a breach regardless of competency.
  • Maintain records of: what training was delivered, who attended, when, by whom, and competency outcome.
  • "We trained them" is only defensible if documented.
PPG context
New warehouse automation equipment requires verified operator training before use. HR must work with HSE to build a training matrix for new systems as part of the AI rollout change management plan.
Fair Work Act — Training in Enterprise Agreements
EA provisions for study leave, allowances, training entitlements
FederalEA provisionsStudy leave
▾
What to know
  • EAs may include paid study leave, training allowances, or employer-funded qualifications — these become enforceable entitlements.
  • If an employee is required to complete mandatory training outside ordinary hours, they may be entitled to overtime or penalty rates.
PPG context
Committing to reskilling provisions in the next EA (2027) could be a strong bargaining offering — reducing union resistance while meeting the legal duty to consult about changes to work systems.
Workforce Planning
Legal and compliance obligations that shape how organisations plan, structure, change, and rightsize their workforce.
Fair Work Act — Major Workplace Change Consultation
s.202–205 and Modern Award consultation term
FederalConsultationStructural change
▾
When consultation is required
  • A major workplace change likely to have significant effect on employees — including technology changes that affect roles.
  • Consultation must be genuine: must occur while the employer can still be influenced. Post-decision notification is not consultation.
Minimum consultation steps
  • Written notice to affected employees as soon as practicable
  • Provide relevant information (nature, effects, timing)
  • Invite employees and representatives to discuss and propose alternatives
  • Genuinely consider input before finalising decisions
PPG context
The AI/automation rollout and planned Perth/Adelaide expansion are both major workplace changes. CEO Marcus Okafor's direct style risks skipping consultation. HR must ensure both changes go through a documented process — critical given 61% union membership in Operations.
Privacy Act 1988 — Workforce Data & Analytics
APPs 3, 6, 10, 11 — collecting and using employee data
FederalHRIS dataPeople analytics
▾
Workforce planning data obligations
  • Workforce analytics data (tenure, salary, performance ratings, skills) is personal information — APPs apply.
  • APP 10 — Ensure data quality: inaccurate HRIS data leads to poor decisions and discrimination risks.
  • APP 6 — Data collected for one purpose (e.g. payroll) cannot be used for another (e.g. AI-based performance scoring) without employee awareness.
PPG context
If PPG uses HRIS data to build predictive flight-risk models, employees should be informed — using data in ways they wouldn't expect is an APP 6 breach.
Superannuation Guarantee (Administration) Act 1992
11.5% employer contribution — rising to 12% July 2025 · Payday super from July 2026
FederalSuperTotal employment cost
▾
Key obligations
  • Employers must contribute 11.5% of ordinary time earnings (FY2025 rate; rises to 12% from 1 July 2025) to an employee's nominated super fund.
  • Applies to all employees regardless of earnings.
  • Payday super from 1 July 2026 — super must be paid on every payday, not just quarterly.
  • Failure to pay = Superannuation Guarantee Charge (SGC) — not tax-deductible, includes penalties.
PPG context
PPG's planned Perth expansion (~60 FTE) adds ~$500K+ in super obligations annually. Workforce plans must include super in total employment cost modelling. Understating headcount costs is a common planning error.
Modern Awards — Road Transport & Logistics
Road Transport and Distribution Award 2020 · Clerks Private Sector Award 2020
Federal AwardClassificationPenalty rates
▾
Why awards matter for workforce planning
  • Award classification structures define how roles are graded and what minimum rates apply.
  • Annual wage reviews by FWC adjust minimum rates — must be built into forward salary cost modelling.
  • Penalty rates, shift allowances, and overtime loadings can significantly increase labour cost above base rate.
PPG context
PPG must model shift rosters against Award penalty rates to accurately project labour costs — especially for a 24/7 operation where penalty rates apply on weekends and public holidays.
