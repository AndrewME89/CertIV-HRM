Mission
To connect Australian businesses to their supply chains efficiently, sustainably, and reliably — delivering smarter logistics through technology and people.
Vision (2028)
Become the most trusted mid-market logistics partner in the Asia-Pacific region, recognised for operational excellence and a workforce that leads the industry in capability and wellbeing.
Strategic pillars
Operational excellence
Reduce cost-to-serve by 12% by FY2027 through process automation and workforce productivity improvements.
Talent & capability
Build a future-ready workforce by investing in upskilling, leadership pipelines, and competitive EVP to attract top logistics talent.
Technology-led growth
Deploy AI-driven route optimisation and warehouse management systems by Q2 FY2026, growing revenue by 15%.
Customer intimacy
Achieve NPS of 65+ by deepening account management relationships and reducing average resolution time by 30%.
Sustainability & ESG
Reach net-zero Scope 1 & 2 emissions by 2030. Link executive remuneration to ESG KPIs from FY2026.
Geographic expansion
Open a fourth hub (Perth or Adelaide) by FY2027, adding ~60 FTE and expanding freight coverage to WA/SA corridors.
HR strategic objectives
1. Reduce voluntary turnover to 12% by FY2027
Redesign EVP, introduce stay interviews, and implement structured 90-day onboarding. Focus on Operations department (currently 22% turnover).
2. Raise engagement score to 75/100 by FY2026
Run quarterly pulse surveys, launch a manager effectiveness program, and create visible career pathways particularly for warehouse and transport staff.
3. Build a leadership pipeline for geographic expansion
Identify 15 high-potential employees by Q3 FY2025, place them in a 12-month accelerator program, and target 80% internal promotion rate for senior roles.
4. Achieve gender pay equity by FY2026
Close the current 9.1% gender pay gap through remuneration audits, transparent banding, and a target of 40% women in management by FY2027 (currently 31%).

Active HR risks & issues
High operations turnover (22%)
The warehouse and operations cohort loses roughly one in five workers annually. Exit interview data points to shift patterns, limited career progression, and manager quality as root causes. Estimated replacement cost: ~$1.2M per year.
AI change management risk
Rollout of warehouse automation and AI route optimisation has created anxiety in Operations. 38% of surveyed workers fear job displacement. No formal change management or reskilling program is currently in place.
Gender pay gap at senior levels
A 9.1% gap at executive level and 7.6% at senior manager level. WGEA reporting obligations apply. Gap is partly structural (fewer women in senior roles) but a remuneration audit has surfaced like-for-like gaps in Tech and Ops.
Tech talent competition
PPG Digital (54 staff) faces 18% turnover as developers and data engineers receive unsolicited offers. Salary benchmarking shows PPG is 8–12% below market for mid-senior tech roles.
Succession gaps in critical roles
Only 52% of critical roles have an identified successor. The CFO (retirement in 18 months) and two Regional Ops Managers have no ready-now successors. Geographic expansion requires leaders that do not currently exist internally.
Engagement below target (67/100)
Pay & recognition (58) and career development (61) are lowest-scoring drivers. Neutral employees represent 44% of the workforce — this cohort is a flight risk if competitors raise wages.
Enterprise Agreement due for renewal FY2027
The current EA expires June 2027. Given inflation and the 2023 renegotiation tensions, early planning for bargaining strategy is recommended. Union membership in Operations sits at 61%.
