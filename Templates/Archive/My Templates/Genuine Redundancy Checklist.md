# Genuine Redundancy Checklist

> **Purpose:** Confirm that a redundancy meets the definition of "genuine redundancy" under s.389 of the *Fair Work Act 2009* — a key defence against unfair dismissal claims.
>
> **Three-part test:** A redundancy is genuine when:
> 1. The job is no longer required due to operational changes;
> 2. Consultation obligations under any applicable Modern Award or EBA have been met; and
> 3. It was not reasonable in all circumstances to redeploy the employee.

# NES Redundancy Pay Scale

> Source: *Fair Work Act 2009*, s.119–120. Applies to employees covered by the NES whose employment ends by reason of genuine redundancy.
>
> **Exclusions:** Small business employers (fewer than 15 employees) are exempt from the NES redundancy pay scale. Casual employees and employees whose employment is less than 12 months are not entitled to redundancy pay.

---

## Redundancy Pay Scale

| Period of Continuous Service | Redundancy Pay (Weeks of Base Pay) |
|---|---|
| At least 1 year but less than 2 years | 4 weeks |
| At least 2 years but less than 3 years | 6 weeks |
| At least 3 years but less than 4 years | 7 weeks |
| At least 4 years but less than 5 years | 8 weeks |
| At least 5 years but less than 6 years | 10 weeks |
| At least 6 years but less than 7 years | 11 weeks |
| At least 7 years but less than 8 years | 13 weeks |
| At least 8 years but less than 9 years | 14 weeks |
| At least 9 years but less than 10 years | 16 weeks |
| At least 10 years | 12 weeks *(reduces at 10 years — long service leave interaction)* |

> **Note:** "Week's pay" = the employee's base rate for their ordinary hours of work (excludes overtime, penalty rates, allowances).

---

## NES Notice of Termination Table

> Source: *Fair Work Act 2009*, s.117. Minimum notice periods. Award, EBA, or contract provisions may provide more generous entitlements — always check.

| Employee's Period of Continuous Service | Minimum Notice Period |
|---|---|
| Less than 1 year | 1 week |
| At least 1 year but less than 3 years | 2 weeks |
| At least 3 years but less than 5 years | 3 weeks |
| At least 5 years or more | 4 weeks |

**Additional:** Add 1 week if the employee is over 45 years old and has completed at least 2 years of continuous service.

> **Note:** Notice can be worked or paid out in lieu (or a combination). Employees must also provide notice of resignation as specified in their contract/Award. For serious misconduct, no notice is required.
