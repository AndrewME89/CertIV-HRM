# Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

> **Purpose:** A PIP is a structured, documented process to give an employee a clear and supported opportunity to address identified performance deficiencies.
>
> **Important:** A PIP should only be commenced after informal performance conversations have occurred and been documented. It is not a precursor to dismissal — it is a genuine opportunity to improve. Consult HR before initiating. Ensure natural justice is observed throughout (employee informed of concerns, given opportunity to respond, given reasonable support and time to improve).

## Part F: Possible Outcomes

At the end of the PIP period, the outcome will be one of the following:

- **✅ PIP closed — improvement achieved:** Performance now meets the required standard. Monitoring ceases. Documented outcome placed on file.
- **⚠️ PIP extended:** Some improvement but standard not fully met. Extension granted with revised goals and timeline.
- **❌ PIP unsuccessful — further action:** Performance has not improved to the required standard. Further disciplinary action up to and including termination may follow. Employee will be given opportunity to respond before any final decision is made.
