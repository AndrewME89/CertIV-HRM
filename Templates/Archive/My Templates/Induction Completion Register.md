# Buddy Program Guide

## Purpose

The buddy program pairs new employees with an experienced colleague for their first 4–8 weeks. The buddy provides social support, answers day-to-day questions, and helps the new starter navigate the culture and environment — not to be confused with formal training or line management.

---

## Buddy Eligibility

A good buddy is someone who:

- Has been with the organisation for at least 12 months
- Is in the same or adjacent team (not the direct manager)
- Has a positive attitude and embodies the organisation's values
- Has the time and willingness to commit to the role
- Volunteers for the role (do not assign reluctant buddies)

---

## Buddy Responsibilities

| Timeframe | Activity |
|---|---|
| **Before Day 1** | Reach out via email/message to welcome the new starter |
| **Day 1** | Meet the new starter, show them around, have lunch together |
| **Week 1** | Check in daily — answer questions, introduce to colleagues, debrief on how things are going |
| **Weeks 2–4** | Weekly catch-up (30 mins). Help with cultural navigation |
| **Weeks 5–8** | Fortnightly check-in. Gradual transition to independence |
| **End of program** | Debrief with manager/HR — any concerns or observations |

---

## Buddy — Not Responsible For

- Performance management
- Formal training delivery
- Acting as a conduit to the manager
- Making employment decisions

---

## Conversation Starters for Buddies

**Week 1:**
- "How was your first day? What went well / what was confusing?"
- "Is there anything you haven't been able to find yet?"
- "Who have you met so far? Want me to introduce you to a few more people?"

**Weeks 2–4:**
- "How are you finding the team dynamic?"
- "Is there anything about how we do things here that seems different from what you expected?"
- "Are you getting what you need from your manager and the broader induction?"
