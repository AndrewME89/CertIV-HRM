# 9-Box Talent Grid

> **Purpose:** The 9-box grid is a talent management tool that plots employees on a 3x3 matrix across two dimensions: **Current Performance** (x-axis) and **Future Potential** (y-axis). It is used in succession planning, talent identification, and development prioritisation.
>
> **Important:** This tool should supplement — not replace — structured performance data. It is inherently subjective. Use consistent definitions, calibrate across managers, and document rationale. Do not use this tool to make compensation or termination decisions in isolation.

---

## The Grid

```
HIGH      │  Box 7        │  Box 8        │  Box 9
POTENTIAL │  Rough Diamond│  Rising Star  │  Star / Future Leader
          │               │               │
──────────┼───────────────┼───────────────┼───────────────
MODERATE  │  Box 4        │  Box 5        │  Box 6
POTENTIAL │  Inconsistent │  Core Player  │  High Performer
          │  Player       │               │
──────────┼───────────────┼───────────────┼───────────────
LOW       │  Box 1        │  Box 2        │  Box 3
POTENTIAL │  Underperformer│ Solid Contributor│ Expert / Specialist
          │               │               │
          └───────────────┴───────────────┴───────────────
               LOW            MODERATE           HIGH
                         CURRENT PERFORMANCE
```

---

## Box Definitions

| Box | Label | Description | Action Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Underperformer | Low performance, limited growth potential. May be in wrong role. | Manage performance; consider role fit or exit |
| **2** | Solid Contributor | Moderate performance, low potential for advancement. Reliable. | Maintain engagement; clarify expectations |
| **3** | Expert / Specialist | High current performance but likely at career ceiling (by choice or design). | Retain through recognition, technical growth, pay |
| **4** | Inconsistent Player | High potential but performance variable. May be disengaged, under-supported, or in wrong role. | Coach, stretch, remove blockers; close watch |
| **5** | Core Player | Solid performer, moderate potential. The backbone of the organisation. | Invest in development; recognise contribution |
| **6** | High Performer | Exceeds current requirements. Some upward potential. | Accelerate growth; expose to stretch roles |
| **7** | Rough Diamond | High potential but not yet performing. May be new, mis-assigned, or underdeveloped. | Invest heavily; assign mentor; remove obstacles |
| **8** | Rising Star | Strong performer with clear leadership/advancement potential. | Fast-track development; expose to leadership |
| **9** | Star / Future Leader | Top performer and top potential. Key succession candidate. | Retain at all costs; align to succession plan |

---

## Definitions: Performance & Potential

### Performance Ratings

| Rating | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **High** | Consistently exceeds role expectations. Delivers above target. Recognised by peers and leaders. |
| **Moderate** | Meets role expectations reliably. Occasionally exceeds. No significant concerns. |
| **Low** | Does not consistently meet role expectations. Performance concerns have been raised. |

### Potential Ratings

| Rating | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **High** | Demonstrates ability to take on greater scope, complexity, or leadership in the near term (1–2 years). Shows learning agility, influence, and vision beyond current role. |
| **Moderate** | Could advance over time (3–5 years) with the right development and opportunity. Not yet demonstrating readiness. |
| **Low** | Likely to remain effective in current or similar role. Little evidence of desire or capacity for advancement. This is not a negative — many roles benefit from deep stability. |

---

## Calibration Session Process

1. **Pre-work:** Managers independently plot their direct reports using the definitions above. Document rationale.
2. **Calibration meeting:** Managers present their placements to the group. Discuss outliers, inconsistencies, and cross-team talent.
3. **Consensus placement:** Agree on final grid placement for each employee. Update rationale.
4. **Action planning:** For each box (especially 7, 8, 9), identify development actions, succession nominations, or retention risks.
5. **Communication:** Decide what (if anything) is shared with employees. Best practice is to share development direction — not the box placement label itself.

## Cautions & Bias Mitigation

Before finalising placements, the panel should ask:

- [ ] Are our "high potential" ratings concentrated in a particular demographic (age, gender, tenure)? Why?
- [ ] Have we conflated "potential" with "confidence" or "presence"? (Common source of gender bias)
- [ ] Have we accounted for employees in new roles who may be underperforming due to transition, not capability?
- [ ] Are we rating potential based on future ability, or on past performance in a different context?
- [ ] Have we checked for recency bias (recent strong/weak performance skewing overall rating)?
