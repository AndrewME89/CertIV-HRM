Dear [First Name],

## First Written Warning — [Brief Description of Issue]

This letter constitutes a **first written warning** regarding your [conduct / performance] as outlined below.

---

### Background

On [date(s)], the following concern(s) were identified:

> [Describe the specific conduct or performance issue clearly and factually. Include dates, incidents, witnesses (if relevant), and how the issue was identified. Avoid emotive language.]

This matter was discussed with you in a meeting held on [date]. [Name of support person], a [support person / union representative], was also present. / You attended without a support person.

---

### Our Expectations

Our expectation is that you [describe the standard, policy, or behaviour expected]. This is reflected in [your contract / the Code of Conduct / the relevant policy / your position description / clause X of the applicable Award or EBA].

Your [conduct / performance] fell below this standard because [specific explanation].

---

### Your Response

At the meeting, you [summarise the employee's response — their explanation or any mitigating factors they raised].

We have considered your response carefully. [State whether the response changed the outcome and why, or why it did not.]

---

### Warning

Having considered all of the above, [Organisation Name] is issuing you with this **first written warning**.

You are required to [state the specific improvement or behavioural change required, with measurable outcomes and a timeframe where possible].

---

### Consequences

If your [conduct / performance] does not improve to the required standard, or if there is a further [conduct / performance] issue, further disciplinary action may follow, up to and including **termination of your employment**.

---

### Next Steps

- A review of your [conduct / performance] against the above expectations will occur on / by [date].
- You are encouraged to speak with your manager or HR if you need support.
- You may wish to access the Employee Assistance Program (EAP): [contact details].

# Show Cause Letter

[Organisation Letterhead]

**PRIVATE & CONFIDENTIAL**

**Date:** ___________________________
**To:** [Employee Full Name]
**Position:** ___________________________

---

Dear [First Name],

## Notice to Show Cause — [Brief Description of Allegation]

I am writing to inform you of serious concerns regarding your [conduct / performance] and to provide you with an opportunity to respond before any decision is made.

---

### Allegations

The following allegations have been made against you / concerns have been identified:

**Allegation 1:**
On or around [date], you are alleged to have [specific allegation — factual, not conclusory].

**Allegation 2 (if applicable):**
[...]

These allegations, if substantiated, may constitute [serious misconduct / a significant performance failure] and may be grounds for disciplinary action up to and including **termination of employment**.

---

### Your Right to Respond

Before any decision is made, you are invited to provide a written response addressing these allegations.

**Your response is due by:** [Date — typically 2–3 business days]
**Please send your response to:** [Name / email / address]

You are encouraged to seek independent advice (e.g., from a union representative, legal advisor, or the Fair Work Ombudsman) before responding.

---

### Your Right to a Support Person

You have the right to have a support person (a colleague, union representative, or other person of your choosing) assist you in any meeting related to this matter. Please advise us if you wish to bring a support person, and we will accommodate this.

---

### Interim Arrangements *(if applicable)*

Pending resolution of this matter, you have been directed to [remain at home on paid/unpaid stand-down / continue working with modified duties / other arrangement]. This is not a finding of misconduct and does not prejudge the outcome.

---

### Next Steps

Once we have received your response (or if no response is received by the due date), a meeting will be scheduled to discuss the matter further. You will be given the opportunity to respond in person at that meeting as well.

We are committed to conducting this process fairly and in accordance with the principles of natural justice.

If you have any questions, please contact [HR contact name] at [email / phone].

Yours sincerely,

___________________________
[Name]
[Title]
[Organisation Name]
