## Risk Rating Matrix (Likelihood × Consequence)

|  | **Consequence 1 — Insignificant** | **2 — Minor** | **3 — Moderate** | **4 — Major** | **5 — Catastrophic** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Likelihood 5 — Almost Certain** | Medium (5) | High (10) | High (15) | Extreme (20) | Extreme (25) |
| **Likelihood 4 — Likely** | Low (4) | Medium (8) | High (12) | High (16) | Extreme (20) |
| **Likelihood 3 — Possible** | Low (3) | Medium (6) | Medium (9) | High (12) | High (15) |
| **Likelihood 2 — Unlikely** | Low (2) | Low (4) | Medium (6) | Medium (8) | High (10) |
| **Likelihood 1 — Rare** | Low (1) | Low (2) | Low (3) | Low (4) | Medium (5) |

**Risk Ratings:**
- **Extreme (15–25):** Immediate action required. Cease activity if necessary. Escalate to executive.
- **High (10–14):** Urgent action. Controls required before work continues.
- **Medium (5–9):** Action required. Assign owner and implement within defined timeframe.
- **Low (1–4):** Monitor. Review at next scheduled hazard register review.

---

## Hierarchy of Controls (apply in order — higher = more effective)

1. **Elimination** — Remove the hazard entirely
2. **Substitution** — Replace with something less hazardous
3. **Isolation** — Separate people from the hazard
4. **Engineering Controls** — Physical controls (guarding, ventilation, etc.)
5. **Administrative Controls** — Procedures, training, supervision
6. **PPE** — Personal Protective Equipment (last resort only)
