# Structured Interview Guide — STAR Method

> **Purpose:** Provide a consistent, legally defensible interview process using the Situation-Task-Action-Result (STAR) behavioural framework.
>
> **Legal note:** Questions must relate to genuine occupational requirements. Avoid any questions touching on age, race, sex, disability, pregnancy, religion, sexual orientation, family/carer responsibilities, or other protected attributes under Australian anti-discrimination law.

## Opening (5 mins)

Use a consistent opening with every candidate:

> *"Thank you for coming in today. My name is [Name] and I'm joined by [Name(s)]. Today we'll be asking you a series of questions about your past experience — we find this gives us the best insight into how you approach your work. There are no trick questions, and we encourage you to take a moment to think before answering. We'll take notes throughout, which helps us give you fair consideration. We'll have time for your questions at the end. Do you have any questions before we start?"*

---

## Part 1: Role Motivation (Not Scored)

These questions establish context and are not scored — they should not influence the scoring matrix.

1. Can you walk me through your current role and what has led you to apply for this position?

2. What attracted you specifically to this role and our organisation?

## Scoring Guide

| Score | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **5 — Exceptional** | Clear, specific example. All STAR elements present. Strong impact/result. Insight into their own role in the outcome. |
| **4 — Strong** | Good example with most STAR elements. Positive result. Minor gaps in detail. |
| **3 — Satisfactory** | Adequate example. Partially meets criteria. Some elements vague or missing. |
| **2 — Weak** | Vague or poorly structured response. Limited evidence of the competency. |
| **1 — Not Demonstrated** | Unable to provide an example, or example does not relate to the competency. |

---

## Questions to Avoid (Anti-Discrimination)

Never ask about:

- Age, date of birth, or year of graduation
- Pregnancy, plans to have children, or family responsibilities
- Relationship or marital status
- Race, ethnicity, or national origin
- Religion or religious observance
- Disability (unless directly related to a genuine occupational requirement, and only after a conditional offer)
- Sexual orientation or gender identity
- Union membership

*If a candidate volunteers this information, do not engage with it or record it.*
